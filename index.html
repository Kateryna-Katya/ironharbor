From Rust to Power

Building a shipyard? It starts with chaos, deadlines, and big dreams. In Iron Harbor, you begin with only a single dock, limited resources, and a few small contracts. But the harbor never sleeps. With each upgrade, each completed vessel, and each new automation line, the whole vibe of the place changes. What starts as this tiny corner operation? Before you know it, it's just growing like crazy.

Look, this isn't your typical builder game. You can actually feel the shipyard coming alive as you play. New sections of the yard open up. Equipment becomes smarter. Ships become larger. Income rises while your systems become more efficient. The best part isn't rushing through everything - it's seeing your shipyard become this massive operation because of all the choices you made along the way.

How the Shipyard Works

Construct Ships

Take on contracts and build a wide range of vessels, from compact utility boats to large industrial cargo ships. Each project brings new demands, new materials, and higher rewards.

Automate Production

Install smarter systems across your harbor. Speed up assembly, reduce delays, optimize output, and let your production lines keep working even while you focus on expanding.

Expand Docks

Unlock new areas of the port and turn empty shoreline into valuable infrastructure. More docks mean more contracts, more traffic, and more room for your harbor to grow.

Upgrade Everything

Boost cranes, improve loading speed, enhance welding stations, modernize ship frames, and turn every part of your yard into a stronger, faster operation.

Idle Progress with Real Weight

What makes this game addictive is of steady progress you can actually see. Every action pushes the harbor forward. Even when you’re away, the shipyard keeps moving. Materials continue to process. Contracts continue to pay. Systems continue to produce. When you return, the place feels alive — fuller, faster, richer.

The joy of the game comes from this balance: easy to pick up, but the upgrades really do something, and you get to watch this whole industrial operation come together piece by piece. It is easy to start, but hard to stop once your harbor begins to scale.
